Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions / Bureau du surintendant des institutions financières

Deposit-taking Institutions (DTIs)

Canadian bank joins G-SIB ranks

On November 22, the Financial Stability Board released its 2019 list of global systemically important banks (G-SIBs) and OSFI confirmed the Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD) G-SIB designation. TD is already subject to OSFI’s framework for domestic systemically important banks (D-SIBs), and therefore is well positioned to meet the G-SIB requirements starting in November 2020.

Operational Risk Capital

OSFI is revising its capital requirements for operational risk applicable to DTIs, reflecting the final Basel III revisions published by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) in December 2017. In advance of these new requirements, institutions currently approved to use the Advanced Measurement Approach (AMA) for operational risk capital are to report using the current Standardized Approach for fiscal year 2020.

Liquidity Principles

In December, OSFI released the final version of its guideline B-6 Liquidity Principles for implementation on January 1, 2020. Guideline B-6 sets out OSFI’s expectations around the management of liquidity risk for the institutions it regulates and complements the minimum liquidity requirements set out in the Liquidity Adequacy Requirements guideline. Guideline B-6 was last updated in 2012. The changes aim to ensure that the guideline remains current, and appropriate for the scale and complexity of institutions.

Covered bonds

OSFI issued a letter Revised Covered Bond Limit Calculation. The revision, which took effect as of August 1, 2019, updates the calculation of the covered bond limit.

Interest Rate Risk Management

At the end of May, OSFI issued the final version of guideline B-12 Interest Rate Risk Management. The guideline incorporates most of the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision’s 2016 guidance on interest rate risk in the banking book (IRRBB), including the methods expected to be used by institutions for measuring, managing and monitoring IRRBB, as well as updates related to supervisory practices. The revised guideline will apply to D-SIBs as of January 1, 2020, and January 1, 2021 for all other DTIs.

Foreign Bank Branch Deposits

In December, OSFI released the final version of guideline A-10 Foreign Bank Branch Deposit Requirement, effective on January 1, 2020. The guideline includes changes to the deposit ratio calculation.

Capital and Liquidity for smaller institutions

In mid-July, OSFI released a discussion paper on its initiative to advance proportionality in the capital and liquidity regime for small and medium-sized DTIs, collectively referred to as SMSBs. The discussion paper sought input on possible changes to capital and liquidity requirements for SMSBs, and outlined OSFI’s initiative to develop more tailored requirements that take into account their unique nature.

DTI survey results

During the 2018-19 fiscal year, OSFI commissioned Sage Research Corporation, an independent research firm, to conduct a confidential consultation with DTIs OSFI regulates. The primary objective of the research was to explore senior executives’ impressions of OSFI in the discharge of a number of key elements of its mandate. A summary of the results of the study are now available on OSFI’s website.
