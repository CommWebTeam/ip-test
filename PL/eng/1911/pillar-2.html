Reassuring Reinsurance

Since OSFI published a discussion paper on its reinsurance framework last year, it has already identified significant evolution of the reinsurance environment and practices from the previous update ten years ago. The objective of the paper was twofold: to deepen an understanding of broader reinsurance practices and collect feedback from industry and participants. The paper sought comments on proposed changes aimed at improving management of large exposures and concentration of counterparties, leveling the playing field, and making adjustments to continue the adequacy of capital requirements in the face of evolution in the sector.

The industry has responded with robust feedback on these proposals as well as further data for analysis. Both the feedback and data have validated the need for revisions to the reinsurance framework allowing OSFI to develop better and more targeted approaches to making the framework more effective while limiting potential disruption. Key refinements to OSFI’s approach include:

  • changing the proposal on large policy limits

  • coordinating the eventual implementation of relevant guidelines given the importance of interconnections deferring elements related to IFRS 17 until it is implemented

The depth of engagement with industry has been effective in refining our proposals and has driven a shared understanding of the reinsurance market, which will help improve the framework. OSFI will hold further consultations in 2020.

OSFI also commits to continue its practice of sharing supervisory expectations and to provide training to industry to improve transparency and institutions’ effectiveness in meeting the requirements once finalized.

OSFI looks forward to working closely with stakeholders to find solutions that continue to deliver on its mandate of protecting policyholders while allowing financial institutions to compete effectively and take reasonable risks.

Comments and questions can be directed to information@osfi-bsif.gc.ca.
