Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions / Bureau du surintendant des institutions financières

Insurance

Insurance Capital Standard

While broadly supportive of the goals of the Insurance Capital Standard (ICS), OSFI did not support the ICS design for a five-year monitoring period, which was proposed at the executive committee meeting of the International Association of Insurance Supervisors (IAIS) in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates.

OSFI’s statement in mid-November explains that the standard in its current form is not fit for purpose for the Canadian market. Specifically, the proposed capital requirements for long-term products are too high to be compatible with OSFI's mandate of allowing Canadian insurers to compete and take reasonable risks.

KPMG Canada 2019 Insurance Conference

On November 28, Neville Henderson, Assistant Superintendent, Insurance Supervision Sector, participated at KPMG Canada's 2019 Insurance Conference. During the fireside chat with Chris Cornell, KPMG Canada's Insurance Sector Lead, Mr. Henderson covered a range of issues, including key trends, global shifts in risks and initiatives that will shape the Canadian and international insurance environments.

International Financial Reporting Standards

The move to IFRS 17 is a significant undertaking by insurers and OSFI. OSFI is seeking views on the draft regulatory forms and instructions updated for IFRS 17. Comments are welcome until March 21, 2020. This lengthy consultation process allows for early identification and mitigation of potential issues.

In mid-August, OSFI released a letter providing an update on the milestones completed and the activities underway as OSFI continues to support a robust implementation of International Financial Reporting Standard (IFRS) 17 - Insurance Contracts.

Mortgage Insurance Advisory

In early-September, OSFI issued an advisory for mortgage insurers regarding capital requirements for insured mortgages issued under the new First-Time Home Buyer Incentive (FTHBI) program.

The advisory, in conjunction with the Mortgage Insurer Capital Adequacy Test (MICAT) guideline, sets out the regulatory framework within which OSFI assesses whether a mortgage insurance company maintains adequate capital for FTHBI insured mortgages.

The advisory came into effect on November 1, 2019.

P&C Risk Management Seminar

On October 28, 2019, OSFI hosted its 11th Annual Risk Management Seminar for property and casualty (P&C) insurers. The seminar provided an opportunity for OSFI officials to engage with 190 representatives from federally regulated P&C companies.

The seminar included discussions on climate risks, organizational culture, updates on IFRS-17 and next steps for the reinsurance review. Opening remarks by Penny Lee, Senior Director, Property and Casualty Insurance Group are available or can be viewed on YouTube.

P&C internal models

OSFI issued for comment a draft guideline E-25, Internal Model Oversight Framework, for property and casualty insurance companies. The guideline outlines OSFI’s expectations for insurers when they establish and maintain an oversight framework for internal models used to determine regulatory capital requirements.
