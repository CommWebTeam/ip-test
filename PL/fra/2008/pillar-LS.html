Domestic Stability Buffer

On December 10, OSFI announced that it had set the level of the Domestic Stability Buffer at 2.25% of total risk-weighted assets, effective April 30, 2020.

The Domestic Stability Buffer contributes to domestic systemically important banks’ (D-SIBs) resilience to key vulnerabilities and system-wide risks, thereby contributing to financial stability.

OSFI reviews and sets the level of the Domestic Stability Buffer on a semi-annual basis (June and December), based on its ongoing monitoring of federally regulated financial institutions as well as system-wide and sectoral developments. Decisions on the calibration of the buffer are based on OSFI supervisory judgment, informed by its monitoring and analytical work on a range of vulnerabilities, and are made in consultation with OSFI’s federal financial regulatory partners.

Annual Report

On December 15, the OSFI Annual Report 2018-2019 was tabled in the House of Commons. The report is a key component of OSFI's accountability framework and provides a summary of OSFI's performance against annual priorities and achievements.

Executive Appointment

On August 19, Superintendent Jeremy Rudin announced the appointment of Sanjiv Talwar as Assistant Superintendent, Risk Support Sector.

Mr. Talwar brings to OSFI more than 25 years’ experience in the financial sector, including senior executive roles in risk management and trading in multinational banking institutions in Canada, Europe and Asia. He was head of Risk Capital, Stress Testing and Model Development at the Bank of Montreal, where he oversaw the determination of capital demand and was accountable for all risk model development and the use of stress testing within BMO. Prior to BMO, Mr. Talwar served in senior positions at OCBC Bank in Singapore and CIBC in Toronto.

Mr. Talwar replaces Ben Gully, who was appointed Assistant Superintendent, Regulation Sector.

Deposit-taking Institutions (DTIs)

Canadian bank joins G-SIB ranks

On November 22, the Financial Stability Board released its 2019 list of global systemically important banks (G-SIBs) and OSFI confirmed the Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD) G-SIB designation. TD is already subject to OSFI’s framework for domestic systemically important banks (D-SIBs), and therefore is well positioned to meet the G-SIB requirements starting in November 2020.

Operational Risk Capital

OSFI is revising its capital requirements for operational risk applicable to DTIs, reflecting the final Basel III revisions published by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) in December 2017. In advance of these new requirements, institutions currently approved to use the Advanced Measurement Approach (AMA) for operational risk capital are to report using the current Standardized Approach for fiscal year 2020.

Liquidity Principles

In December, OSFI released the final version of its guideline B-6 Liquidity Principles for implementation on January 1, 2020. Guideline B-6 sets out OSFI’s expectations around the management of liquidity risk for the institutions it regulates and complements the minimum liquidity requirements set out in the Liquidity Adequacy Requirements guideline. Guideline B-6 was last updated in 2012. The changes aim to ensure that the guideline remains current, and appropriate for the scale and complexity of institutions.

Covered bonds

OSFI issued a letter Revised Covered Bond Limit Calculation. The revision, which took effect as of August 1, 2019, updates the calculation of the covered bond limit.

Interest Rate Risk Management

At the end of May, OSFI issued the final version of guideline B-12 Interest Rate Risk Management. The guideline incorporates most of the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision’s 2016 guidance on interest rate risk in the banking book (IRRBB), including the methods expected to be used by institutions for measuring, managing and monitoring IRRBB, as well as updates related to supervisory practices. The revised guideline will apply to D-SIBs as of January 1, 2020, and January 1, 2021 for all other DTIs.

Foreign Bank Branch Deposits

In December, OSFI released the final version of guideline A-10 Foreign Bank Branch Deposit Requirement, effective on January 1, 2020. The guideline includes changes to the deposit ratio calculation.

Capital and Liquidity for smaller institutions

In mid-July, OSFI released a discussion paper on its initiative to advance proportionality in the capital and liquidity regime for small and medium-sized DTIs, collectively referred to as SMSBs. The discussion paper sought input on possible changes to capital and liquidity requirements for SMSBs, and outlined OSFI’s initiative to develop more tailored requirements that take into account their unique nature.

DTI survey results

During the 2018-19 fiscal year, OSFI commissioned Sage Research Corporation, an independent research firm, to conduct a confidential consultation with DTIs OSFI regulates. The primary objective of the research was to explore senior executives’ impressions of OSFI in the discharge of a number of key elements of its mandate. A summary of the results of the study are now available on OSFI’s website.

Insurance

Insurance Capital Standard

While broadly supportive of the goals of the Insurance Capital Standard (ICS), OSFI did not support the ICS design for a five-year monitoring period, which was proposed at the executive committee meeting of the International Association of Insurance Supervisors (IAIS) in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates.

OSFI’s statement in mid-November explains that the standard in its current form is not fit for purpose for the Canadian market. Specifically, the proposed capital requirements for long-term products are too high to be compatible with OSFI's mandate of allowing Canadian insurers to compete and take reasonable risks.

KPMG Canada 2019 Insurance Conference

On November 28, Neville Henderson, Assistant Superintendent, Insurance Supervision Sector, participated at KPMG Canada's 2019 Insurance Conference. During the fireside chat with Chris Cornell, KPMG Canada's Insurance Sector Lead, Mr. Henderson covered a range of issues, including key trends, global shifts in risks and initiatives that will shape the Canadian and international insurance environments.

International Financial Reporting Standards

The move to IFRS 17 is a significant undertaking by insurers and OSFI. OSFI is seeking views on the draft regulatory forms and instructions updated for IFRS 17. Comments are welcome until March 21, 2020. This lengthy consultation process allows for early identification and mitigation of potential issues.

In mid-August, OSFI released a letter providing an update on the milestones completed and the activities underway as OSFI continues to support a robust implementation of International Financial Reporting Standard (IFRS) 17 - Insurance Contracts.

Mortgage Insurance Advisory

In early-September, OSFI issued an advisory for mortgage insurers regarding capital requirements for insured mortgages issued under the new First-Time Home Buyer Incentive (FTHBI) program.

The advisory, in conjunction with the Mortgage Insurer Capital Adequacy Test (MICAT) guideline, sets out the regulatory framework within which OSFI assesses whether a mortgage insurance company maintains adequate capital for FTHBI insured mortgages.

The advisory came into effect on November 1, 2019.

P&C Risk Management Seminar

On October 28, 2019, OSFI hosted its 11th Annual Risk Management Seminar for property and casualty (P&C) insurers. The seminar provided an opportunity for OSFI officials to engage with 190 representatives from federally regulated P&C companies.

The seminar included discussions on climate risks, organizational culture, updates on IFRS-17 and next steps for the reinsurance review. Opening remarks by Penny Lee, Senior Director, Property and Casualty Insurance Group are available or can be viewed on YouTube.

P&C internal models

OSFI issued for comment a draft guideline E-25, Internal Model Oversight Framework, for property and casualty insurance companies. The guideline outlines OSFI’s expectations for insurers when they establish and maintain an oversight framework for internal models used to determine regulatory capital requirements.

Other Topics

OSFI survey results

In the fall of 2018, Phoenix Strategic Perspectives, an independent research firm, conducted a confidential survey on OSFI’s behalf with executives of the financial institutions that OSFI regulates to explore perceptions of OSFI’s performance in the discharge of a number of key elements of its mandate. A summary of the results of the study are now available on OSFI’s website.

CPAB-OSFI roundtable

The Canadian Public Accountability Board (CPAB) and OSFI co-hosted a roundtable on audit quality in Canada in the fall. The event was an opportunity for more than forty of Canada’s public accounting and regulatory leaders to discuss ways to support continued financial system stability by enhancing public confidence in audit quality.

Margin requirements

OSFI published a letter in late June to provide guidance on the BCBS-IOSCO statement on margin requirements related to transitioning away from interbank offer rates and on-boarding of phase 4 and 5 counterparties for initial margin requirements.

Securities Administration requests

Effective December 9, 2019, the Regulatory Affairs Division in Ottawa will begin processing all Securities Administration Unit requests. This letter explains OSFI’s reasoning behind the change, efficiencies to be gained and what stakeholders can expect.

Ted Price

OSFI employees were saddened to learn that former Deputy Superintendent Edward (Ted) Price died on October 8. Ted joined OSFI from the private sector in 2001 and appointed Assistant Superintendent, Supervision in 2006. In 2011, he was named Deputy Superintendent. He retired from OSFI in July 2013. Ted's leadership through the 2008 financial crisis and its aftermath served OSFI and Canada well.
